Champagne
Showers

A ritual of excess. Eleven courses, one podium, midnight.
II · Arrival

The door does not
open for everyone.

A sealed marble vestibule. The room beyond is rumor until you are inside it.

III · The Crown

A hundred coupes,
suspended.

The first thing you look up at. Five tiers, hand-cut crystal, lit from within. A toast you can stand under.

IV · The Cellar

Every bottle has
a name on it.

A passage lined in members' reserves. Walk it slowly. Yours is in here somewhere.

V · The Scales

A quieter corner.

Brass scales catch every candle. Velvet absorbs the conversation. The room is loud but here is not.

VI · The Bar

Forty seats.
One pyramid.

A brass-lit horseshoe. House Moët, vintage rooms, and a bartender who knows your year.

VII · The Bubble

Twenty-eight orbs.
One booth.

The room's most photographed table. Reserved by name, not by hour.

VIII

The Floor.

IX · The Cube

Glass on four sides.
One ritual inside.

Champagne is sprayed at eleven. The cube contains it. The room remembers it.

X · The Kit

Suit up.

Sponsored ponchos. Slippers. Towel service. Champagne on ice before the first course is poured.

XI · The Threshold

Private rooms.
Private rituals.

Six dressing booths. Velvet behind every door. The bar is louder when you cannot hear it yet.

XII · In Camera

A room of one's own.

Brushed brass. Quilted velvet. Two hangers and a long mirror. The last quiet moment of the night.

XIII · The Crossing

Suited up.
The walk back is yours.

The bridge lifts you above the floor. The room watches you return — kitted, ready, lit from below.

XIV · The Pivot

Dinner ends.
Something else begins.

The room turns at eleven. Plates retire. The seal lights up. The DJ steps in.

XV · On Grid

The race is dinner.

Goggles down. Bottles up. The cube is lit; the table is set.

XVI · Round One

The procession.

Berets. Gloves. Dom. A round is announced; the room rises.

XVII · The Pop

Aim. Pop.
Knock it down.

Brass cup pyramids on black marble. House rules. House champagne. No prize except the noise.

XVIII · The Bath

For birthdays.
For audacity.

A solid brass tub. Four bearers. One guest of honor. The room parts and the bottles go up.

XIX

Eleven o'clock.

XX · Curtain

Until the last coupe.
